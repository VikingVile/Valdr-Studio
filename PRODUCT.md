# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Small-to-mid local businesses, creators, and B2B/industrial clients evaluating Valdr Studio as a web design/development partner (examples already in the portfolio: a city transit app, a musicians' songbook app, a personal trainer, a CNC manufacturing company). They land on this site while comparing studios or freelancers, sizing up credibility and technical competence largely from the site's own execution — this site is Valdr's own portfolio piece, so its quality directly stands in for the quality of work a client would receive. The job to be done: quickly judge "is this studio legit, technically sharp, and premium enough for my project" and then act — book a strategy call or reach out directly.

## Product Purpose

Win new client work for Valdr Studio by demonstrating premium web design/dev capability through the site's own execution. The site doesn't just describe the service, it performs it. Success looks like a visitor trusting the studio's competence within the first scroll and converting to a booked call or direct contact.

## Brand Personality

Serious, technical, premium, quietly confident. Not playful, not corporate-generic. Confidence is communicated through precision and restraint, not through hype copy or visual loudness.

## Anti-references

- **Generic AI-template SaaS.** Gradient blobs, cream/beige neutral backgrounds, identical icon-in-a-circle card grids, a tiny uppercase tracked eyebrow above every single section — the current site already leans on some of this pattern (see AUDIT.md/CLAUDE.md history) and it's an explicit thing to keep steering away from, not just avoid on day one.
- **Corporate agency stock-photo polish.** Generic stock imagery and interchangeable "we deliver excellence" agency copy — reads as every other web agency site, undercuts the "quietly confident, technical" personality.
- **Playful/startup consumer app aesthetic.** Bright colors, rounded mascot-style illustration, bouncy/elastic motion — too casual for a serious B2B-facing premium brand.
- Beyond these three named lanes, use ordinary design judgment; no blanket rule beyond them.

## Design Principles

1. **The site is the pitch.** Every interaction is itself evidence of the technical/design competence being sold. A generic pattern or visible rough edge here undercuts the sales pitch directly, more than it would on an ordinary client site.
2. **Quiet confidence over loud selling.** Avoid overstatement, hype language, and visual shouting (oversized claims, urgency gimmicks, look-at-me effects). Let precision and restraint carry the premium feel.
3. **Technical credibility through detail.** The audience is specifically evaluating technical competence, so small craft details — consistent tokens, deliberate motion easing, typographic discipline — matter more here than flashy one-off gimmicks.
4. **B2B trust signals over consumer persuasion tactics.** No countdown timers, fake urgency, or engagement-bait patterns. B2B buyers respond to clarity and proof of work, not consumer-app conversion tricks.
5. **Bilingual parity is a first-class constraint.** EN and SR are equally primary on the homepage. Design and content changes should be checked against both, not tuned for one locale's copy length/rhythm at the other's expense.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No formal WCAG target set for this project (deliberate choice, not an oversight) — apply ordinary good practice (reasonable contrast, keyboard-operable interactive elements) without checking against a specific bar.
